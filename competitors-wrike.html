Click the time tracker icon in a task, or open up the time tracker panel to get started tracking time in Workep. In Wrike, you have to download a Chrome extension and go through the setup process.

You don’t have to take any extra steps to sync Workep with your Google Drive — it’s completely automatic. In Wrike, you have to manually activate the integration with Drive.

Google Calendar is your native calendar in Workep, so your project calendars sync and update back and forth automatically. In Wrike, you have to manually sync projects and tasks one way to Calendar.

Get instant assistance and answers to your questions through Workep´s live chat support. No need to read through lists of articles to get the answers you need fast!

Share your Workep projects with anyone outside of your organization — no signup or extra steps required! Anyone you want to share your Wrike projects with has to sign up and create an account.

Assign an hourly value to your team members to sum up the budget spent working on each task in Workep. Wrike doesn’t have an in-app budget tracker, so you have to use additional tools.

Use Workep’s automated Gantt chart to view your all your tasks laid out against a timeline, create dependencies, and export it to a spreadsheet to use for reporting. There is no Gantt chart view in Wrike.
